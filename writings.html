Writing Pieces

July 11, 2025 The Green Grass All Around Reflection, too late, has brought me the only grief I have felt since your passing. I am disgraced by my disregard for your legacy. Years on, I look back at your image, tainted; mere shadows of your once glowing hair. I had once cherished the days I could see you anew. I had once appreciated your life, but even your calming presence could not persist beyond death. Now, I recall your body betraying you. I see your hair darkened by your fragmented form. Your skin dried and falling remembered as a sickening stick on my skin. But were you not the one that lay by my side as I struggled to sleep? Did you not care for your family members, both old and new? Was it not you who entertained with your joyful trot? To forget what you meant is to forget your life. As you laid motionless in your bed, I did not recognize your imprint on my mind. As your figure was framed by the dirt, I could not see the green grass all around. I can now.
